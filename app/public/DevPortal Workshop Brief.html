Vision Arc: 2024 → 2026

How the DevPortal strategy evolved — what changed, what stayed anchored, what shipped, and what's still ahead.

2024 · Early
Unified Portal Concept
Fragmented tools (Scout, AIW, GED) surface a shared pain: no coherent home. Design sprint launches to define a unified Developer Portal.
→
2024 · Late
Bronze / Silver / Gold
PDX Design formalises phased unification. Bronze = shared nav & SSO. Silver = lift-and-shift AIW. Gold = native workbench integration. JTBD personas defined.
→
2025
Platform Architecture + Experience Principles
Platform-as-a-platform doc defines horizontal kernel + vertical workbenches. Delivery Team becomes the ownership unit. March 2025 design research crystallises the core UX principles: 80/20 paved paths, intent-based tasks, proactive anticipation, and explicit guardrails for what DevPortal should not do.
→
2026 · Now
Agentic & Federated
Agentic Chat (multi-agent orchestration), MCP server in alpha, federated microfrontend shell (Context SDK), and 4 DDE production MCPs. Portal is reframing as ambient intelligence, not a destination.
What Changed

Destination → Ambient. Early vision = one portal URL. Current = intelligence surfaces where devs already work (IDE, CLI, Slack).

Workbench = lift-and-shift. Silver now means iframed AIW, not deeper integration. Reframed Feb 2026 after cost/complexity tradeoffs.

Search → Discovery + Intelligence. Keyword search evolved into semantic retrieval (Glean + Knowledge Graph) and agentic synthesis.

Portal governance → Plugin governance. AppFabric debate: top-down vs. bottom-up is now the active tension.

What Stayed

Single source of truth for developer knowledge remains the north star across every document.

Delivery Team as ownership unit. Introduced in 2025, still the foundational accountability construct.

80/20 paved paths. Solve the 80% of common workflows with opinionated defaults; allow customisation for the remaining 20%. Introduced in 2025, never contested.

JTBD persona framework. The 5 archetypes (AI Newcomer, AI Specialist, Integration Engineer, SRE, EM) still drive research and design decisions.

Federated, not monolithic. Every architecture proposal maintains teams own their vertical; the platform provides shared capabilities only.

What Shipped

Scout integration complete — only vertical fully onboarded to Bronze as of early 2026.

DDE 4 production MCPs — data-discovery, dde-access, databricks, auth. Reduced data-discovery from 45 min → 4 min.

CDC Capability Plugin launched April 2026. First AppFabric plugin in production.

Agentic Chat alpha with Gateway, M&A, and SRE agents. DevPortal MCP server in alpha alongside it.

Knowledge Graph (Neo4j) + Glean semantic layer operational as the intelligence foundation.

Still Ahead

16 of 17 verticals remain at Bronze or below. Silver and Gold for GED, AIW, and others is still the pending majority of the roadmap.

GenUX / GenOS clarification. Whether DevPortal builds its own generative UX layer or delegates to GenOS is an open architecture decision.

A2A (Agent-to-Agent) protocol. Multi-agent coordination beyond current monolithic orchestration — planned but not designed.

Persona-driven IA at Gold. The 5 JTBD role-based navigation and personalized surfaces remain a Gold-phase deliverable.

Day 0 Plugin. IDE-embedded guided onboarding workflow for first-day engineers — still in concept phase.

Research Signal & Unsolved Challenges

What 20+ developer voices tell us, mapped against the current vision — and the gaps that haven't closed yet.

Search & Discovery
Critical · 7 reports
"I have to search five different places before I find what I need — search results are full of outdated docs."
The Knowledge Graph + Glean layer is the direct architectural response. Not yet production-wide.
Documentation Quality
Critical · 6 reports
"Documentation is either missing or so out of date it's misleading. You end up asking colleagues instead."
Autura / GitHub→Autura pipeline is the intended fix. Adoption and maintenance culture remains unsolved.
Onboarding
Critical · 6 reports
"Setting up my first GenAI agent required reading 8 different documents. There was no single start-here path."
AI Newcomer JTBD satisfaction: 32/100 — lowest of all personas. Day 0 Plugin concept is the proposed answer.
AI Tooling Friction
High · 4 reports
"30% of support threads are auth/access errors. You're blocked for 1–7 days before you can even run code."
Only 26% of GenAI use cases have done evaluations — a UX guidance gap, not a capability gap. Median 3 days to first working agent via GenUX.
Reliability & Trust
High · 5 reports
"When the portal goes down, I don't even know where to look. There's no status, no fallback."
DDE MCPs have known silent auth failures. 100-result hard cap with no pagination. Trust gaps undermine adoption even when capability exists.
Positive Signal
Noted · 3 reports
"The DDE MCP cut my data discovery time from 45 minutes to under 5. That's the kind of thing that makes me actually use a tool."
Speed improvements and proactive surfacing are praised when they work. The 45→4 min example is the strongest product proof point available.

Unsolved Challenges — Vision Hasn't Closed These Gaps

Documentation Maintenance Culture

Tooling (Autura pipeline) exists, but there's no incentive structure or ownership model ensuring teams keep docs fresh. Research shows stale docs are as damaging as missing ones.

Auth / Access Friction at Day 0

30% of AIW support threads are auth errors; 1–7 day unblocking times. Silver-phase portal integration doesn't address access provisioning — it only moves the iframe.

Evaluation Culture for GenAI

Only 26% of GenAI use cases have run evaluations. This is a UX guidance gap — developers don't know how or why to evaluate. The vision focuses on tooling, not the workflow habits around it.

Cross-Vertical Navigation (JTBD → IA)

Satisfaction is 32–48/100 across all personas. Persona-driven IA is a Gold-phase goal, but research shows the pain is Silver-phase urgent. There's a phasing mismatch.

Reliability & Silent Failures

DDE MCPs have a 100-result hard cap with no pagination and silent auth failures. Production gaps in today's shipped tooling erode trust even as the vision accelerates forward.

Silver / Gold Reset

Amit Soni flagged: "The Silver/Gold breakdown needs a reset." The FY26 targets ($4M observability savings, >80% CES) require a clearer path than current phase definitions provide.

Design Guardrails — What DevPortal Should Not Do March 2025 research

Don't try to do everything

DevPortal should not include every possible tool a developer might need. Feature sprawl creates navigation complexity and erodes the value of what's actually curated. Scope discipline is a product decision, not a gap.

Don't clone what external tools already do well

It should not hide the tools it builds or replicate features already covered by GitHub, Jira, VS Code, or other external tools. Wrapping external tools in portal chrome adds a login step without adding value.

Don't create more context switching

Avoid causing more tab-switching or platform-hopping. If a DevPortal feature requires a developer to leave their current workflow to use the portal, the integration model needs rethinking — not the developer's habits.

Don't create documentation for documentation's sake

Documentation that nobody reads is worse than no documentation — it creates false confidence and clutters search results. Documentation must be discoverable, maintained, and tied to a real user task.

Don't force users into a single way of working

The 80/20 rule applies: paved paths for the common case, flexibility for edge cases. The portal should guide without mandating — one workflow fits all is an anti-pattern for a platform serving 5 distinct JTBD personas.

04

Future State Brainstorm

Designing for a world where most developers never visit a portal URL — they live in IDE, CLI, and chat. What does DevPortal become in that world?

6 months 1 year

The Portal Becomes a Knowledge API

DevPortal's web UI recedes. Its true product is a high-fidelity, always-current knowledge graph that any IDE plugin, CLI tool, or AI agent can query. Developers never navigate to it — their tools do.

"The 45→4 minute data discovery improvement came from the MCP, not the portal UI. That's the direction."
6 months

IDE as the Primary Interface

The Day 0 Plugin becomes the canonical first experience. VS Code / Cursor sidebar surfaces onboarding flows, compliance checks (HCAP warnings), and discovery — without leaving the editor. The portal URL is a fallback, not the default.

"8 documents to set up a first agent — that workflow needs to live inside the IDE, not across tabs."
6 months 1 year

Agents as First-Class Platform Citizens

A2A (Agent-to-Agent) protocol enables DevPortal to dispatch specialized agents (SRE, M&A, onboarding) that coordinate without human handoffs. Developer intent → outcome, with the portal as orchestration layer only.

"The multi-agent architecture needs more serious commitment — this is at least a 2-year play." — CJ leadership
6 months 1 year

Intent-Based Task Flows

Developers declare intent ("I want to build a GenAI agent", "I need to onboard a new service") and the portal assembles the right sequence of steps, tools, and docs for that specific goal — rather than leaving users to navigate a feature tree.

March 2025 research principle: "Prioritise intent-based tasks and provide modular building blocks." Aligns directly with the Day 0 Plugin concept and paved-path philosophy.
1 year

Ambient Intelligence in the Developer Flow

Instead of developers pulling information, the platform pushes relevant context proactively — PDLC stage warnings in the PR, compliance nudges in the pipeline, cost anomaly alerts in Slack. Zero portal visits required.

"Personalise the developer experience by anticipating needs and issues proactively." — March 2025 research principle
6 months 1 year

Self-Healing Documentation

Agents monitor code merges and API changes, then auto-propose doc updates via Autura. Human review becomes the exception, not the rule. Stale docs — the #1 research complaint — become structurally impossible.

"Documentation is either missing or so out of date it's misleading."
1 year

Composable Workbench for Any Vertical

The Gold-phase vision: any vertical team publishes a plugin via AppFabric that composes into the portal shell with zero custom integration work. The portal becomes a runtime, not a destination.

Requires resolving the top-down vs. bottom-up plugin governance debate before any Gold-phase work can begin.
6 months 1 year

Persona-Adaptive Surfaces

When a developer does open DevPortal, the experience is fully role-aware: AI Newcomer sees onboarding paths and evaluation guides; SRE sees reliability dashboards; EM sees team health. One URL, five different products.

Satisfaction scores (32–48/100) improve immediately when context is role-relevant. This is a Silver/Gold crossover opportunity.

The Core Design Tension

The portal needs to be excellent enough that developers choose to use it — while also being invisible enough that developers don't need to. These aren't contradictory: the portal is the orchestration brain; the IDE and CLI are the hands.

The Design Question for This Workshop

If most tasks move to IDE and CLI in 12 months, which portal surfaces justify investment? What is irreducibly valuable about the web UI — and what should we consciously let go of?
